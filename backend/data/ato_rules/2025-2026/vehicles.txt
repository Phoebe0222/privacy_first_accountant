Vehicle and Travel Expenses

You can claim car expenses when you use your car for work purposes, but not for travel between home and work (ordinary commuting) unless you are required to carry bulky tools.

Work-related car trips include:
- Driving between two workplaces
- Driving to client meetings, job sites, or conferences
- Carrying heavy or bulky equipment that cannot be stored at work

Two methods for claiming car expenses:

1. Cents per kilometre method
Rate: 88 cents per kilometre (2024-25); the 2025-26 rate is updated annually by the ATO.
Maximum: 5,000 kilometres per year per vehicle.
No written evidence of kilometres required, but you need to be able to demonstrate you travelled that distance for work.

2. Logbook method
Keep a logbook for a continuous 12-week period to determine the business-use percentage.
The logbook is valid for 5 years (unless your pattern of use changes by more than 10%).
Claim actual vehicle expenses (fuel, insurance, registration, repairs, depreciation) multiplied by the business-use percentage.
Requires odometer readings at start and end of each income year.

You cannot claim:
- Normal home-to-work travel
- Travel for private purposes
- Parking fines

Other travel:
- Public transport, taxis, ride-share for work travel: claim actual cost.
- Overnight work travel: accommodation, meals (with limits), and incidentals are deductible.
- Travel allowances: if you receive an allowance within ATO reasonable amounts, you may not need written evidence for meals.
