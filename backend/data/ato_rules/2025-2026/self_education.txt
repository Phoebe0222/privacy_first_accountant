Self-Education Expenses

You can claim self-education expenses if the education directly relates to your current employment or business, and it either:
- Maintains or improves skills or knowledge required in your current work, OR
- Results in (or is likely to result in) an increase in income from your current work.

You cannot claim if the education:
- Relates to a new career or future employment, not your current job.
- Provides only general skills not specific to your income-earning activities.

Deductible self-education expenses include:
- Course fees (TAFE, university, private college — but not HECS-HELP repayments)
- Textbooks, stationery, and course materials
- Student union fees (if compulsory)
- Travel to and from education (if the course is directly related to your work)
- Decline in value of a computer or tablet used for study (work-related portion)
- Internet and phone costs used for study (work-related portion)
- Equipment and tools used for the course

You cannot claim:
- HECS-HELP repayments
- Meals (unless an allowable travel deduction)
- Childcare while studying
- Repayment of student loans

Professional development and conferences:
Costs to attend seminars, workshops, webinars, or conferences that directly relate to your current work are deductible — including registration fees, travel, and accommodation.

Records required:
- Receipts and invoices for all expenses.
- Evidence of how the course relates to your current employment (e.g. course outline, employment contract).
