Zone and Overseas Forces Tax Offsets

Zone tax offset:
- Available if your usual place of residence was in a remote area of Australia designated by the ATO as "Zone A", "Zone B", or a "special area" within those zones.
- Eligibility generally requires you lived or worked in the zone for more than 183 days in the income year — or, if you didn't reach 183 days in any single year, the days can be added together across the current and immediately preceding income years to reach 183 days.
- The offset amount depends on which zone applies (Zone A is higher than Zone B, and special areas attract a higher amount again) and whether you have eligible dependants.
- Check the ATO's zone list (by postcode/locality) for the current year, as zone boundaries and special-area listings can be updated.

Overseas forces tax offset:
- Available to Australian Defence Force and Federal Police personnel deployed on an eligible overseas operation (as declared by the relevant Minister), calculated per day of eligible service.
- Income earned for service on some eligible operations may also be exempt from income tax entirely under a separate exemption (in which case the offset doesn't apply to that income, since it's not taxed in the first place).

Both offsets are claimed through your tax return and are non-refundable.
