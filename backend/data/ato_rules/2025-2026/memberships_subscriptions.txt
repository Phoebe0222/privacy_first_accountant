Memberships, Accreditations, Fees and Commissions

You can claim a deduction for memberships, accreditations, fees and commissions that directly relate to earning your employment income.

Deductible:
- Union fees and subscriptions — fully deductible, even if the union also provides personal benefits (e.g. discounts, insurance).
- Professional association or trade union membership fees, where membership relates to your current employment.
- Subscriptions to trade, business or professional journals, magazines and digital publications relevant to your work.
- Annual practising certificate, licence or registration renewal fees required to continue performing your current role (e.g. teacher registration, trade licence renewal, professional body annual fees).
- Commission paid to an agent or manager for managing your income-earning activities (common for entertainers, artists, models, athletes).

Not deductible:
- The initial cost of obtaining a qualification, licence or registration needed to start a new job or change occupations — this is a "cost of getting work", not a cost of doing your current job.
- Memberships to social, sporting or recreational clubs (e.g. gyms, golf clubs), even if you make business contacts there — these are private in nature.
- Subscriptions or memberships that are predominantly for personal interest rather than your income-earning activities.

Apportionment: if a membership or subscription has both work-related and private elements (e.g. a professional association membership that also gives discounted gym access), you can only claim the work-related portion.

Keep invoices, receipts or membership statements as evidence — the $300 substantiation-free threshold for "other work-related expenses" still applies in total across all such claims.
