Tools, Equipment, and Other Assets

You can claim a deduction for tools and equipment you use for work.

Immediate deduction (items costing $300 or less):
If an item costs $300 or less and is used wholly or mainly for work, you can claim the full cost in the year you buy it.
If the item is also used privately, you can only claim the work-related portion, and only if that portion is $300 or less.

Decline in value (depreciation) for items over $300:
You must depreciate items costing more than $300 over their effective life (as determined by the ATO).
Common effective lives: laptops 3 years, smartphones 3 years, cameras 5 years, power tools 7–13 years.
Use the prime cost or diminishing value method consistently for each asset.

Small business instant asset write-off:
Small business entities (aggregated turnover < $10 million) can immediately deduct the business portion of eligible depreciating assets.
The threshold and eligibility rules are set annually in the federal budget — confirm the current threshold for 2025-26.

Repairs vs. improvements:
Repairs to existing tools and equipment are deductible in the year incurred.
Improvements that extend the life or increase the value of an asset must be depreciated.

Computers and technology:
Laptops, tablets, monitors, and external hard drives used for work: deductible (depreciate if over $300).
Work-related software subscriptions: fully deductible in year incurred.
Software purchased outright (not subscription): depreciate if over $300.

Records required:
Receipt or invoice for each item.
For mixed private/work use: record of work-use percentage with supporting evidence.
