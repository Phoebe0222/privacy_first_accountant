Small Business Income Tax Offset and Digital Games Tax Offset

Small business income tax offset (unincorporated small business tax offset):
- Available to individuals — sole traders, and partners or beneficiaries who receive a share of small business income through a partnership or trust — where the business has an aggregated turnover under $5 million.
- The offset equals 16% of the tax payable on your "total net small business income" (your share of business income, less deductions attributable to it).
- Capped at $1,000 per individual per year.
- Calculated automatically by the ATO from your tax return — no separate application required. Non-refundable (reduces tax payable to a minimum of $0).
- Does NOT apply to companies — companies instead benefit from the lower "base rate entity" company tax rate, where eligible.

Digital games tax offset:
- A refundable tax offset for companies that carry out eligible digital game development activities in Australia.
- Offset = 30% of eligible Qualifying Australian Development Expenditure (QADE), subject to a minimum spend threshold and an annual cap per company/game.
- Requires a certificate confirming the game qualifies (from the relevant government cultural body) before claiming via the company tax return.
- Niche concession — only relevant to companies in digital game development; not applicable to most sole traders or small businesses.

Other concessions, offsets and rebates (covered separately or on the ATO concessions overview page) include small business restructure rollover relief, small business CGT concessions, and various industry-specific rebates — these are added or changed periodically, so check current availability for 2025-26.
