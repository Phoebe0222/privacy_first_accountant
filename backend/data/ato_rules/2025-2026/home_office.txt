Home Office Expenses

You can claim a deduction for additional expenses you incur when working from home. There are two methods:

1. Fixed Rate Method (67 cents per hour from 1 July 2022 onward)
Covers: electricity and gas for heating, cooling, and lighting; internet; phone; stationery and computer consumables; and decline in value of furniture and equipment used for work.
You must keep a record of hours worked from home (a timesheet, diary, or similar).
You cannot separately claim internet or phone if using this method — they are already included.

2. Actual Cost Method
Claim the actual work-related portion of each expense separately.
Requires detailed records: receipts, bills, and a reasonable basis for apportionment.
You can also claim decline in value (depreciation) of home office equipment and furniture under this method.

What you cannot claim under either method:
- Mortgage interest, rent, or rates (unless you carry on a business from home with a dedicated space)
- Coffee, tea, milk for home consumption
- General household items

Dedicated home office (running a business from home):
If you have a room set aside exclusively and regularly for business, you may be able to claim occupancy expenses (rent, mortgage interest, rates) apportioned by floor area and time used.
You must have a genuine, permanent place of business at home — not just occasional work.

Decline in value (depreciation) of equipment:
Assets costing more than $300 must be depreciated over their effective life.
Assets costing $300 or less used exclusively for work can be claimed in full immediately.
Small business entities may use simplified depreciation rules (instant asset write-off).
