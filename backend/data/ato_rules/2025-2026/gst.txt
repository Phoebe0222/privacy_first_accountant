GST — Goods and Services Tax

Registration:
You must register for GST if your business has a GST turnover of $75,000 or more (or $150,000 for non-profit organisations).
If your turnover is below $75,000, registration is optional but recommended if you make taxable supplies.
Ride-share and taxi drivers must register regardless of turnover.

GST rate: 10% on taxable supplies.

GST-free supplies (no GST charged, but input tax credits can still be claimed):
- Basic food (fresh food, bread, milk — but not restaurant meals or hot food)
- Medical, health, and educational services
- Exports of goods and services
- Childcare
- Water, sewerage, and sewerage

Input-taxed supplies (no GST charged, no input tax credits on related costs):
- Financial supplies (lending money, issuing shares)
- Residential rent
- Selling residential property (under certain conditions)

Claiming input tax credits (ITCs):
You can claim ITCs for the GST included in business purchases if:
- You are registered for GST.
- The purchase is used for your business (taxable) activities.
- You hold a tax invoice for purchases over $82.50 (GST-inclusive).

Tax invoices must include: supplier's ABN, description of the supply, GST amount (or statement that price includes GST), date, and total price.

BAS lodgement:
Report GST collected (1A) and ITCs claimed (1B) on your Business Activity Statement.
Net GST payable = 1A − 1B.
Lodge quarterly (or monthly if elected) by the due date — typically 28 days after the quarter ends.

Simplified GST accounting methods for small business:
- Business norms method: estimate the business-use percentage based on ATO-published norms.
- Snapshot method: calculate actual business use over a representative period.
