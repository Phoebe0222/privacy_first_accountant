Phone and Internet Expenses

You can claim a deduction for the work-related portion of your phone and internet expenses.

If you use your phone or internet for both work and private purposes, you must apportion the expense. Claim only the work-related portion.

Methods of apportionment:
1. Keep a 4-week diary to determine your typical work-use percentage, then apply that percentage to the full year.
2. Count the number of work calls or data used for work as a proportion of total usage.

Phone bills:
- Monthly plan (phone calls and data): claim work-use percentage.
- If your employer pays or reimburses you, you cannot claim a deduction.
- If you use the fixed rate home office method (67c/hour), phone expenses are already included — do not claim separately.

Internet:
- Home internet: claim work-use percentage only.
- If you use the 67c/hour fixed rate, internet is already covered — do not double-claim.

Employer-provided phone or plan:
If your employer provides the phone or pays the bill, you generally cannot claim a deduction for those costs.

Evidence required:
- Itemised bill for at least one representative 4-week period.
- Diary or log showing how you apportioned the expense.
- If claiming less than $50 for phone and internet combined, no written evidence is required (estimate must be reasonable).
